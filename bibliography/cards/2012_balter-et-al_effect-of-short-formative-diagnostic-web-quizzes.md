## Riassunto in italiano

Lo studio ha esaminato se la combinazione di quiz online brevi con domande generiche e solo feedback binario (giusto/sbagliato) fosse benefica per l'apprendimento degli studenti. I quiz sono stati implementati in tre classi su due materie diverse presso università svedesi e statunitensi durante le prime settimane del corso. Quasi tutti gli studenti hanno apprezzato i quiz; il 38% ha modificato la propria valutazione della conoscenza acquisita, mentre oltre il 20% ha alterato le proprie abitudini di studio (studiando più intensamente o prima). Lo studio conclude che brevi quiz con domande generiche e feedback limitato su ogni domanda producono effetti positivi quando somministrati all'inizio dei corsi.

## Abstract in English

To help students gauge their understanding of basic concepts and encourage good study habits, we administered short online quizzes that use generic questions in the crucial first few weeks of a course. The purpose of the study was to investigate whether the combination of these web quizzes with generic questions with only binary feedback (right or wrong) would be beneficial for students' learning. We implemented these quizzes in three classes in two different subjects at two different universities, one in Sweden and one in the USA. Almost all students appreciated having these quizzes and 38% of them changed their view on how much they knew of the material covered in the course. Furthermore, over 20% of the students reported altering their study habits as a consequence, in particular studying harder or earlier. In conclusion, this study indicated that short quizzes using generic questions with limited correct/incorrect feedback on each question, have positive effects when administered early in courses.

## Metodologia

Case study involving pilot studies and implementation in three different courses at two universities: KTH Royal Institute of Technology (Stockholm, Sweden) and Williams College (Williamstown, MA, USA). The method involved administering short online quizzes with generic questions using variables ('Slots and Fillers') allowing unlimited retakes. Feedback was limited to binary correct/incorrect per question. Quizzes were offered weekly for the first 3-4 weeks of full semester courses. Data collection included anonymous online surveys (8 questions) after mid-term, voluntary interviews (telephone/Skype/f2f), and teacher interviews.

## Risultati principali

1. Almost all students appreciated the non-mandatory nature of the quizzes.
2. 38% of students adjusted their self-evaluation of knowledge based on quiz results (either realizing they knew more or less than thought).
3. Over 20% changed study habits, mostly studying harder or earlier.
4. The option to retake quizzes repeatedly was appreciated by almost all students (>92%).
5. Most participants did not report increased stress; only ~1/3 of KTH students felt slightly more stressed due to the disappearance of quiz access after a deadline.

## Conclusioni

Short online diagnostic quizzes with generic questions and minimal feedback have positive effects when administered early in courses. They encourage good study habits, improve self-assessment accuracy, and can be implemented without extensive resources for automatic feedback construction compared to systems providing detailed explanations. The combination of generic questions and short quizzes is valuable for those hesitant about the resource needs of full automatic formative assessment.
