## Riassunto in italiano

L'articolo presenta ELIZA, un programma per computer progettato per simulare una conversazione in linguaggio naturale con un utente umano. Il programma, implementato sul sistema MAC time-sharing del MIT, utilizza regole di decomposizione e riassemblaggio basate su parole chiave per generare risposte apparentemente intelligenti. Weizenbaum discute i problemi tecnici fondamentali affrontati da ELIZA, tra cui l'identificazione delle parole chiave, la scoperta del contesto minimo, la scelta di trasformazioni appropriate e la generazione di risposte in assenza di parole chiave. Viene illustrato come il programma manipoli il testo in ingresso per produrre risposte che sembrano comprendere le emozioni e le relazioni dell'utente, pur operando attraverso meccanismi procedurali semplici. L'articolo esplora anche le implicazioni psicologiche di tale approccio e le limitazioni tecniche imposte dalla necessità di economizzare tempo di calcolo e spazio di memoria.

## Abstract in English

This paper presents ELIZA, a computer program designed to simulate natural language conversation with a human user. Implemented on the MIT MAC time-sharing system, the program uses decomposition and reassembly rules based on keywords to generate seemingly intelligent responses. Weizenbaum discusses the fundamental technical problems addressed by ELIZA, including keyword identification, discovery of minimal context, selection of appropriate transformations, and response generation in the absence of keywords. The article illustrates how the program manipulates input text to produce responses that appear to understand the user's emotions and relationships, despite operating through simple procedural mechanisms. The paper also explores the psychological implications of this approach and the technical limitations imposed by the need to conserve computer time and storage space.

<!-- field:methodology -->
## Metodologia

The study involves the implementation and analysis of the ELIZA program, which operates on the MAC time-sharing system at MIT. The program uses a set of decomposition rules triggered by keywords in the input text and reassembly rules to generate responses. The methodology includes a discussion of the technical problems addressed by ELIZA, such as keyword identification, context discovery, transformation selection, and response generation. The paper also includes a sample conversation between a user and ELIZA to illustrate the program's capabilities and limitations.

ELIZA can simulate natural language conversation by using pattern matching and transformation rules. The program appears to understand the user's emotions and relationships, but this is achieved through simple procedural mechanisms. ## Dati rilevanti

ELIZA is a program which makes natural language conversation with a Computer possible. Its present implementation is on the MAC time-sharing system at MIT. It is written in SLIP [4] for the IBM 7091. Its name was chosen to emphasize that it may be incrementally improved by its users, since its language abilities may be continually improved by a "teacher".
