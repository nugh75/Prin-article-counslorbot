## Riassunto in italiano

Obiettivo: Esplorare l'uso del feedback nei questionari interattivi basati su Web per raccogliere dati sui fattori di stile di vita negli studi epidemiologici. Design: Viene riportato uno studio di coorte su fattori di stile di vita e infezioni delle vie respiratorie superiori tra 1805 uomini e donne, in cui è stato introdotto l'interattività tramite feedback personalizzato e a livello di gruppo riguardo all'assunzione dietetica, attività fisica ed incidenza delle infezioni come incentivi per i partecipanti. Risultati: Il feedback personalizzato era offerto nel questionario basale; il 88% dei partecipanti ha scelto attivamente di riceverlo almeno una volta. I tassi di compliance nei follow-up sono stati dell'83-84%, con un totale del 74% che completò tutti i cinque follow-up, risultando più alto tra chi aveva scelto il feedback basale.

<!-- field:abstractEn -->
## Abstract in English

Objective: We explored the use of feedback in interactive web-based questionnaires for collecting data on lifestyle factors in epidemiological studies. Design: Here we report from a cohort study on lifestyle factors and upper respiratory tract infections among 1805 men and women. We introduced interactivity in the form of personalized feedback and feedback on a group level regarding dietary intake, physical activity and incidence of infections in web-based questionnaires as incentives for the respondents to continue answering questions and stay in the study. Results: Personalized feedback was offered in the baseline questionnaire and feedback on a group level in the five follow-up questionnaires. In total, 88 % of the participants actively chose to get personalized feedback at least once in the baseline questionnaire. The follow-up questionnaires were sent by email and the overall compliance at each follow-up was 83–84 %, despite only one reminder. In total, 74 % completed all five follow-ups.

<!-- field:researchObjectives -->
## Obiettivi della ricerca

To show the proportion of participants who chose to view personalized feedback in the baseline questionnaire and completing the follow-up questionnaires, which type of feedback was mostly used, and if there were any age and gender differences.

<!-- field:researchQuestions -->
## Domande di ricerca

[1. What is the proportion of participants choosing personalized feedback?; 2. Are there associations between actively choosing feedback and compliance?; 3. Which types of feedback are most used?; 4. Are there age or gender differences in feedback usage?

<!-- field:methodology -->
## Metodologia

Population-based cohort study (Lifestyle and Immune function - LIME) using interactive web questionnaires administered via email.

<!-- field:sample -->
## Campione / partecipanti

[Men and women, aged 20–60 years, residing in a middle-sized county in Sweden. Initially 5000 individuals randomly selected; 1805 completed baseline questionnaire; 1745 invited to follow-up after exclusions (n=60).]

<!-- field:instruments -->
## Strumenti e fonti dei dati

[Interactive web-based questionnaires with personalized feedback options based on self-reported answers regarding BMI, energy expenditure, nutrient intake (vitamin C, Ca, Fe, fibre), and meal composition. FFQ (96-item) for food habits; Perceived Stress Scale by Cohen et al.; Physical activity assessment method.

<!-- field:mainFindings -->
## Risultati principali

[88% of participants actively chose personalized feedback at baseline. Overall compliance per follow-up was 83-84%. Total completion rate for all five follow-ups was 74%. Compliance was higher among those who chose feedback in the baseline questionnaire (chose three times: 76%; chose once or more: 73%) compared to those who never chose feedback (58%). Energy expenditure from physical activity was the most frequently chosen type of feedback.

<!-- field:conclusions -->
## Conclusioni

[It is possible to use feedback in web questionnaires and it has potential to increase compliance. The majority of participants actively chose personalized feedback, suggesting future research should focus on improving feedback design to achieve even higher compliance.]

<!-- field:limitations -->
## Limiti

[Technical error prevented data collection for BMI feedback choice; exclusion of respondents without email or with filtered emails (junk mail); lack of a control group receiving no feedback due to resource constraints and ethical considerations regarding recruitment independence from interest in feedback.

<!-- field:implications -->
## Implicazioni teoriche e pratiche

[Feedback can serve as a non-economic incentive to improve retention in longitudinal web-based studies. Future designs should consider visual vs text-based feedback formats.]
